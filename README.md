How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?:

Interpreting user needs begins with actively listening to the frusturations and communication they are trying to provide. User's will mostly describe what they want in general terms, It is then up to the developer to sit down and figure out how they incorporate this into user stories for development purposes. This helps us see more of what our requirements and acceptance criteria are in the long run for any project.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?:

While in this class I have grown to like the incremental style of developing code, which is what Agile-Scrum methodology supports. For long projects or any project in general I see this approach being extremely beneficial and will be my main source of style when I am working on future projects. It allows for mistakes to be caught earlier on rather than finishing a project and having to restart which seems to me to be much more efficient.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?:

To me being a good team member relies heavily on the communication. From working on projects to a change in acceptance criteria or requirements, communication must stay strong through it all or the project will fail and fall apart. Working while in school has given me quite a bit of insight as to how communication plays such a big part in most of the downfalls teams have throughout their day and the same goes for software development.
